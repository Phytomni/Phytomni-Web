## 1. Step-by-Step Quantitative RT-PCR Protocol

### 1. Sample Pooling

1. **Pipette 60 μl each of the 10 clinical NP/OP swabs into a single 1.5-ml microcentrifuge tube.** The total volume is 600 μl. The original volume of the specimens ranged from 1 ml to 3 ml.
2. **Aliquot 300 μl pooled swabs to each of two clean 1.5-ml microcentrifuge tubes** and label with the same sample ID.

### 2. Preparation of Positive Sample-Pool

1. **Identify a positive NP/OP swab** that has a Cr value ≥15.00 but ≤36.00.
2. **Aliquot the identified positive NP/OP swab** and store at -20℃.
3. **Prepare the positive sample-pool** according to Steps A2-A3 by mixing 1 positive with 9 unknown samples.

### 3. RNA Extraction

1. **Place the dried VB column in a clean 1.5-ml microcentrifuge tube.**
2. **Add 35 μl DNAse RNase-free water** to the center of the VB column matrix and incubate for 3 min at room temperature (15-25℃).
3. **Centrifuge at ≥6,000 ×g for 1 min** to elute the purified nucleic acid.
4. **Pipette 20 μl Proteinase K** into each 1.5-ml microcentrifuge tube.
5. **Add 200 μl Buffer AL** and mix thoroughly by shaking vigorously.
6. **Incubate at 56℃ for 10 min.**
7. **Add 200 μl ethanol (96-100%)** and mix thoroughly by shaking vigorously.
8. **Pipette 700 μl mixture into a DNeasy Mini spin column** placed in a 2-ml collection tube. Centrifuge at ≥6,000 ×g for 1 min. Repeat Step (v) until all the mixture (two 1.5-ml microcentrifuge tubes prepared in Step A3) has completely flowed through the spin column. Discard the flowthrough and collection tube.
9. **Place the spin column into a clean 2-ml collection tube.**
10. **Add 500 μl Buffer AW1** and centrifuge for 1 min at ≥6,000×g.
11. **Discard the flowthrough and collection tube.**
12. **Place the spin column in a clean 2-ml collection tube, add 500 μl Buffer AW2,** and centrifuge for 3 min at 14,000×g.
13. **Discard the flowthrough and collection tube.**
14. **Transfer the spin column into a clean 1.5-ml microcentrifuge tube.**
15. **Elute the RNA** by adding 35 μl DNAse-and RNAse-free water to the center of the spin column membrane. Incubate for 3 min at room temperature (15-25℃).
16. **Centrifuge for 1 min at ≥6,000 ×g** to elute the purified nucleic acid.

### 4. RT-qPCR

1. **Prepare the RdRP-Primer/Probe mixture (10 μM)** (see Recipe).
2. **Prepare 3 tests:** (1) positive sample-pool; (2) non-template control (NTC); and (3) unknown sample-pool.
3. **Prepare the RT-qPCR mixture** using the SuperScriptTM III One-Step RT-PCR System with PlatinumTM Taq DNA Polymerase as described in Table 2.
   - **Reagent | Final concentration | Volume (μl) for one reaction**
   - 2x reaction buffer | 1x | 10.0
   - SuperScriptTM III RT/PlatinumTM Taq Mix | - | 0.5
   - RdRP-Primer/Probe mixture (10 μM) | 2 μM | 1.0
   - Purified nucleic acid (10 sample pool) | - | 8.5
   - **Total volume** | - | 20.0
   - Replace purified nucleic acid with DNAse- and RNAse-free water for NTC.
4. **Run the RT-qPCR cycles** as described below:
   - 55℃ for 15 min
   - 95℃ for 2 min
   - 95℃ for 15 s and 56℃ for 30 s for 50 cycles

### 5. Data Analysis

1. **Interpret and record the results** using the criteria below:
   - If the Cr value is ≤14.00, consider it as contamination. Repeat the RT-qPCR of the pooled purified nucleic acid. Upon detection of the same Cr value in the repeated RT-qPCR, deconvolute the positive pool(s) and repeat the nucleic acid extraction and RT-qPCR on individual samples from the positive pool(s). Test results are confirmed by a conventional RT-qPCR clinical test on a single sample.
   - If the Cr value is >14.01 but ≤45, record the Cr value and consider positive detection of SARS-CoV-2 in the pooled samples. Deconvolute the positive pool(s) and repeat the nucleic acid extraction and RT-qPCR on individual samples from the positive pool(s). Test results are confirmed by a conventional RT-qPCR clinical test on a single sample.
   - If the Cr value is ≥45.01 or no Cr value is detected, record the pooled samples as negative. Each single sample is reported as negative in this pool.

### 6. Primer Mix Preparation

1. **Prepare each one of the primers and probes at 100 μM.**
2. **Primer/probe mix (100 μl):**

3. **Aliquot and store at -20℃.**

  ## 2. Step-by-Step Western Blotting Protocol

### Western Blotting Protocol

#### 1. Preparation of Protein Samples

1. **Prepare the loading buffer**: Mix the loading buffer (4×) and NuPAGE™ LDS sample buffer (4x) in a 1:1 volume.
2. **Denature the protein samples**: Apply the mix to the purified protein of mouse IRS1 (0.2–0.4 μg/μL) and IRS2 (0.2–0.5 μg/μL) in 1.5 mL Eppendorf tubes, then incubate at 95 ℃ for 10 minutes. Place the tubes on ice immediately after heating.
3. **Prepare the gel**: Prepare a polyacrylamide gel with a 5% stacking and a 12% separating gel, 0.75 mm thickness.
4. **Load the samples**: Add the required amount of 5x SDS sample buffer to the protein extracts and incubate at 95 ℃ for 5 minutes. Load the prestained protein ladder (4 μL) and 8 μL of the extract into the slots of the gel in the electrophoresis chamber filled with electrophoresis buffer.
5. **Run the gel**: Run the gel at 200 V for 1 hour.

#### 2. Protein Transfer

1. **Prepare the membrane**: Pre-wet the PVDF membrane in methanol for 20 seconds, then rinse quickly in ultrapure water and soak in transfer buffer for at least 15 minutes.
2. **Assemble the transfer sandwich**: After electrophoresis, wash the electrophoresis plates with pure water, place the gel over the blotting paper, carefully place the pre-wet PVDF membrane onto the gel, place a second blotting paper onto the PVDF membrane, and place a sponge support pad.
3. **Transfer the proteins**: Perform the transfer at 100 V for 2 hours at room temperature or 10 mA/gel overnight at 4 ℃ using a wet transfer method.

#### 3. Blocking and Incubation

1. **Block the membrane**: Incubate the membrane with 10 mL of blocking solution (5% milk in PBST) for 1 hour at room temperature on a rolling device.
2. **Denature and renature the proteins**: Incubate the membrane in denaturing and renaturing buffers with varying concentrations of guanidine-HCl as shown in Table 1. Finally, incubate the membrane in the denaturing and renaturing buffer without guanidine-HCl overnight at 4 ℃.

#### 4. Detection of Protein-Protein Interactions

1. **Incubate with IRβ protein**: Apply 3–10 μg of IRβ in PBST buffer containing 3% milk and incubate with the membrane at 4 ℃ overnight.
2. **Wash the membrane**: Wash off unbound IRβ with PBST buffer three times for 10 minutes.
3. **Incubate with primary antibody**: Incubate with anti-IRβ (1:250 dilution) in 3% milk PBST at 4 ℃ overnight.
4. **Wash the membrane**: Wash the membrane with PBST buffer three times for 10 minutes.
5. **Incubate with secondary antibody**: Incubate the membrane with the secondary antibody (1:5,000 dilution) for 1 hour at room temperature.
6. **Final wash**: Wash the membrane with PBST buffer three times for 10 minutes, then rinse with PBS for 5 minutes.

#### 5. Visualization

1. **Prepare the chemiluminescent substrate**: Mix the detection reagent 1 and 2 at a 1:1 volume.
2. **Incubate the blot**: Incubate the blot with the chemiluminescent substrate for 2 minutes.
3. **Image the blot**: Remove the blot from the substrate and place it in the ChemiDoc XRS+ gel imaging system to capture the image. Ensure the blot is completely wet with the chemiluminescent substrate and does not dry out (0.1 mL/cm²).

#### Recipes

1. **Loading buffer**: 50 mM Tris-HCl (pH 6.8), 2% sodium dodecyl sulfate, 10% glycerol, 1% β-mercaptoethanol, 12.5 mM EDTA, 0.02% bromophenol blue.
2. **Wet transfer buffer**: 25 mM Tris, 192 mM glycine, 20% methanol.
3. **Denaturing and renaturing buffers**: Varying concentrations of guanidine-HCl as shown in Table 1.
4. **Protein-binding buffer**: 100 mM NaCl, 20 mM Tris (pH 7.6), 0.5 mM EDTA, 10% glycerol, 0.1% Tween-20, 2% skim milk powder, 1 mM DTT.
5. **PBST buffer**: 4 mM KH2PO4, 16 mM Na2HPO4, 115 mM NaCl (pH 7.4), 0.05% Tween-20.

#### Equipment

1. Mini gel tank (Thermo Fisher Scientific, catalog number: A25977)
2. PowerPac™ basic power supply (Bio-Rad, catalog number: 1645050)
3. ChemiDoc XRS+ gel imaging system (Bio-Rad)
4. Mini trans-blot electrophoretic transfer cell (Bio-Rad, catalog number: 1703930)
5. Immuno-Blot PVDF membrane (Bio-Rad, catalog number: 1620177)

#### Notes

- Ensure no air bubbles are present in the transfer sandwich.
- The mixture of loading buffer and NuPAGE™ LDS sample buffer (final 1×) can be used as a negative control.
- The use of Easy Blocker is essential to decrease the reactivity of IgG against protein A in the samples.
- The antibody dilutions and incubation times should be optimized based on the specific antibodies used.

This protocol is designed to detect the binding of insulin receptor substrate (IRS) to the insulin receptor (IRβ) using far-western blotting, which is crucial for understanding the insulin signaling pathway.

## 3. Step-by-Step Chromatin Immunoprecipitation (ChIP) Protocol

# Chromatin Immunoprecipitation (ChIP) Protocol for Detecting Direct and Indirect Protein-DNA Interactions in Magnaporthe oryzae

## 1. Vector Preparation

1. **Linearize the pHZ126 Vector**:
   
   - Digest 100 μg of pHZ126 vector DNA with Xhol.
   - Confirm size on a 0.8% agarose gel.
   - Purify using the QIAquick gel extraction kit.

2. **Target Gene Amplification**:
   
   - Design primers with sequences specific to the target gene and 3x FLAG coding sequence.
   - Perform PCR with the following conditions: 35 cycles, melting temperature of 65°C, ideal GC content of 40-60%.
   - Purify the PCR product using the QIAquick gel extraction kit.

3. **Subclone into pHZ126**:
   
   - Co-transform the PCR product into yeast strain XK1-25 using the Alkali-Cation Yeast Transformation Kit.
   - Screen yeast transformants on SD-Trp solid medium.
   - Verify positive colonies using colony PCR.

4. **Extract Yeast Plasmid DNA**:
   
   - Use the Zymoprep Yeast Plasmid Mini Prep II kit.
   - Transform yeast plasmid DNA into E. coli DH5a.
   - Select on ampicillin-containing LB agar plates and verify using colony PCR.
   - Extract plasmid DNA using the Mo Bio UltraClean Midi Plasmid Prep kit and sequence to confirm fidelity.

## 2. Integration of Target Gene into M. oryzae Genome

1. **Grow Mycelia**:
   
   - Grow fungal strains in 350 ml liquid complete medium for 48 h at 24°C with shaking.
   - Harvest mycelia using Miracloth and wash with sterile water.

2. **Protoplast Generation**:
   
   - Incubate mycelia in OM buffer, overlay with ST buffer, and centrifuge.
   - Recover protoplasts from the interface, wash with STC buffer, and resuspend to a concentration of 2-5 x 10^8 protoplasts/ml.

3. **Transformation**:
   
   - Mix 100 μl of protoplasts with 5 μg plasmid DNA.
   - Add PTC buffer and incubate.
   - Mix with molten bottom medium, pour into plates, and overlay with top medium containing Hygromycin B.
   - Incubate until colonies appear.

4. **Screen Transformants**:
   
   - Verify integration using PCR and qRT-PCR with gene-specific primers.
   - Confirm expression using immunoblot analysis with ANTI-FLAG antibody.

## 3. Preparation of Fungal Mycelia for ChIP

1. **Grow Fungal Strains**:
   
   - Grow strains on agar plates for 5-10 days.
   - Excise the leading edge and blend with 350 ml liquid complete medium.
   - Incubate for 48 h at 24°C with shaking.

2. **Collect Mycelia**:
   
   - Harvest using Miracloth, wash extensively with sterile water.
   - Flash freeze in liquid nitrogen and store at -80°C.

## 4. Two-Step Cross-Linking

1. **First Step Cross-Linking**:
   
   - Suspend 1 g of mycelia in 100 ml DSG cross-linking buffer.
   - Incubate for 45 min at room temperature with shaking.

2. **Second Step Cross-Linking**:
   
   - Add formaldehyde to a final concentration of 1% for the last 20 min.

3. **Terminate Cross-Linking**:
   
   - Add glycine to a final concentration of 0.125 M and incubate for 10 min.
   - Wash mycelia with sterile water, flash freeze, and store at -80°C.

## 5. Isolation of Cross-Linked Chromatin

1. **Grind Mycelia**:
   
   - Grind frozen mycelia to a fine powder in liquid nitrogen.

2. **Suspend in 1x NIB**:
   
   - Suspend 100 mg powder in 500 μl 1x NIB and vortex.

3. **Nuclear Extraction**:
   
   - Use CelLytic™ PN Plant Nuclear Isolation Kit following the manufacturer's protocol.
   - Perform all steps at 4°C with pre-cooled buffers.

## 6. Chromatin Shearing by Sonication

1. **Suspend in Nuclear Lysis Buffer**:
   
   - Resuspend crude nuclear fraction in 300 μl nuclear lysis buffer.

2. **Sonicate**:
   
   - Use VirTis VirSonic 50 Ultrasonic Cell Disruptor.
   - Set power to 4, cycles of 20 sec "on" and 20 sec "off" for a total of 6 min.
   - Check sonication efficiency on a 1.5% agarose gel.

## 7. Chromatin Immunoprecipitation

1. **Pre-Clear Chromatin**:
   
   - Incubate with 30 μl Sepharose 4B beads for 4 h at 4°C.
   - Collect supernatant as input chromatin control.

2. **Prepare Affinity Resin**:
   
   - Wash 20 μl ANTI-FLAG M2 Affinity Gel and 20 μl Anti-IgG Agarose with HBS and 0.1 M glycine HCI (pH 3.5).

3. **Incubate with Resin**:
   
   - Incubate 100 μl cleared nuclear lysate with resin overnight at 4°C with gentle agitation.

4. **Wash Resin**:
   
   - Wash four times with HBS containing 0.1% Triton X-100.

5. **Elute**:
   
   - Add 100 μl Elution buffer and incubate for 30 min at 4°C.
   - Collect supernatant.

## 8. Reverse Cross-Linking

1. **Proteinase K Treatment**:
   
   - Add Proteinase K to a final concentration of 0.2 mg/ml and incubate at 45°C for 2 h.

2. **Reverse Cross-Linking**:
   
   - Add NaCl to a final concentration of 0.2 M and incubate at 65°C overnight.

## 9. DNA Purification and Analysis

1. **Remove Contaminating RNA**:
   
   - Incubate with RNase A (0.1 mg/ml) at room temperature for 30 min.

2. **Purify DNA**:
   
   - Use Wizard PCR Clean-up kit and elute in 30 μl ddH2O.

3. **qPCR Analysis**:
   
   - Use specific primers for the gene of interest.
   - Normalize against input control and subtract background signal from Anti-IgG treated sample.

## 10. Data Analysis

1. **Calculate Enrichment**:
   
   - Subtract background and normalize against input.
   - Perform statistical analysis using Student's t-test.

2. **Plot Results**:
   
   - Present relative fold enrichment and standard deviation.

## Reagents and Buffers

- **DSG Cross-Linking Buffer**: 20 mM HEPES (pH 7.4), 1 mM EDTA, 1 mM PMSF, 2 mM DSG.
- **1x NIB**: Dilute 4x NIB with deionized H2O, add DTT to 1 mM.
- **Nuclear Lysis Buffer**: 50 mM Tris-HCl (pH 8.0), 10 mM EDTA, 1% SDS, 1 tablet of protease inhibitor.
- **Elution Buffer**: 50 mM Tris-HCl (pH 7.5), 200 μg/ml 3x FLAG peptide, 150 mM NaCl.
- **HBS**: 10 mM HEPES (pH 7.5), 150 mM NaCl.

## Equipment

- Mini-Tube Rotators (Thermo Fisher Scientific)
- VirTis VirSonic 50 Ultrasonic Cell Disruptor
- CelLytic™ PN Plant Nuclear Isolation Kit
- QIAquick gel extraction kit
- Zymoprep Yeast Plasmid Mini Prep II kit
- Mo Bio UltraClean Midi Plasmid Prep kit

This protocol provides a comprehensive guide for performing ChIP to study protein-DNA interactions in Magnaporthe oryzae, focusing on both direct and indirect interactions using a two-step cross-linking strategy.

## 4. Step-by-Step CRISPR/Cas9 Genome Editing Protocol

# CRISPR/Cas9 Genome Editing Protocol

## 1. Design and Order Oligos

1. Use the web application tool CRISPR-P to choose sgRNA (http://crispr.hzau.edu.cn/CRISPR/).
2. Select "Start Design" and choose the target genome [Arabidopsis thaliana (TAIR10)].
3. Enter the locus tag (e.g., At3g57880 for AtMCTP3).
4. Choose the best location to target your gene of interest. Refer to the sgRNA target on the gene map (highlighted in yellow).
5. The guide score must be as close to 100% as possible (the higher the score, the more effective and specific the sgRNA is); we recommend a 98% cutoff guide score value for an effective and specific sgRNA.
6. Avoid sgRNA with possible off-target effects; if this is not possible, proscribe the potential off-target in exon (refer to the number of off-target sites).
7. Identify the 20-nucleotide sequence of the selected protospacer [refer to guide sequence; the PAM (protospacer adjacent motif) sequence is highlighted in green].

## 2. Annealing of sgRNA Oligos

1. Mix 10 μL of the forward and reverse oligos (100 μM) of each sgRNA in separate microtubes.
2. Incubate the microtubes at 95°C for 5 minutes in a heating block or thermal cycler.
3. Allow the microtubes to slowly cool down to room temperature.
4. Make a 1:200 dilution of the annealed mixture with deionized water.

## 3. Golden Gate Assembly

### 3.1. Linearize the STU CRISPR-Cas9 Plasmid

1. Linearize the STU CRISPR-Cas9 plasmid pTX171 or pTX172 with Bsal.
   - pTX171 or pTX172 (100 ng/μL): 1 μL
   - 10x Cutsmart buffer: 5 μL
   - Bsal (10 U/μL): 1 μL
   - ddH2O: up to 50 μL
2. Incubate at 37°C for 2-4 hours.
3. Load digestion products onto a 1% agarose gel for electrophoresis.
4. Purify the digested vector using the AxyPrep™ DNA Gel Extraction Kit, and quantify DNA concentration using NanoDrop.

### 3.2. Set Up Golden Gate Reaction

1. Set up a Golden Gate reaction for cloning sgRNAs into the STU Cas9 expression vector.
   - pTX171 or pTX172 (100 ng/μL): 1 μL
   - Diluted annealed oligos: 2 μL
   - 10x T4 DNA ligase buffer: 2 μL
   - T4 DNA ligase (400 U/μL): 1 μL
   - Bsal (10 U/μL): 1 μL
   - ddH2O: up to 20 μL
2. Incubate Golden Gate reactions in a thermal cycler using the following program:
   - 10 cycles of 5 minutes at 37°C and 10 minutes at 16°C, then heat to 37°C for another 5 minutes and 80°C for 10 minutes.
3. Transform 5 μL of the reaction into 50 μL competent DH5a cells.
4. Spread the transformed cells on LB plates supplemented with 50 mg/L kanamycin and incubate overnight at 37°C.
5. Verify the positive clones by colony PCR and Sanger sequencing.

## 4. Preparation of Cas9 Ribonucleoproteins

### 4.1. Expression and Purification of SpCas9 Protein

1. Thaw chemically competent Tuner (DE3) cells on ice.
2. Add 1 ng of SpCas9 expression plasmid (Addgene) to 50 μL of competent cells.
3. Mix well by tapping and incubate the tube on ice for 20 minutes.
4. Place the tube in a 42°C water bath for 60 seconds to heat shock E. coli.
5. Incubate the tube back on ice for 2 minutes.
6. Add 500 μL of Terrific Broth to the E. coli and mix well by tapping.
7. Recover E. coli at 37°C for 1 hour with shaking.
8. Spread 100 μL of the cells on a 10-cm LB agar plate containing 50 μg/mL of kanamycin with sterile glass beads until the plate is fully dried.
9. Empty out the beads and incubate the plate at 37°C for 18-20 hours for E. coli colonies to form.

### 4.2. Set Up Starter E. coli Culture

1. Add 2.5 mL of 20x modified M9 buffer and 102 μL of 50 mg/mL kanamycin solution to the 100-mL LB+glycerol medium to reconstitute the complete medium.
2. Inoculate 10 mL LB medium containing 50 μg/mL kanamycin in a conical tube with a single colony from the plate.
3. Grow to saturation overnight at 37°C with shaking at 200 rpm.
4. The next morning, inoculate 200 mL of LB medium containing kanamycin in a flask with 2 mL saturated overnight culture.
5. Grow the cells at 37°C with shaking at 200 rpm to mid-log phase (to an OD600 of approximately 0.4-0.5).
6. Add 160 μL of 1 M IPTG (0.8 mM final concentration) and incubate the cells overnight at 18°C with shaking at 200 rpm.
7. Transfer the culture to a 50 mL conical centrifuge tube and pellet the cells by centrifuging for 20 minutes at 3,000 x g and 4°C. Discard the supernatant and repeat the harvest in the same tube.
8. Resuspend the cell pellets in 10 mL lysis buffer.
9. Add 20 mg lysozyme and 100 μL of 100 mM PMSF (1 mM final concentration).
10. Incubate 1 hour on ice.
11. Sonicate the cells in a conical tube on ice (amplitude 25%, 1 sec ON/1 sec OFF, pulse 30 times). Repeat the sonication process 5 times.
12. Filter the supernatant through a 0.45-μm syringe filter and collect it in a 50 mL conical tube.
13. Load the Ni-NTA and E. coli lysate slurry into the column by pipetting. Discard the flow-through.
14. Wash the column with 400 mL of Lysis buffer.
15. Wash the column with 400 mL of Wash buffer.
16. Add Elution buffer to the column by pipetting and collect a series of 5-mL fractions in 15-mL tubes.
17. Check each fraction by Bradford assay by sampling 5 μL of eluate from each fraction and mixing with 50 μL of 1x Bradford reagent in a 96-well plate.
18. Combine the fractions with a substantial amount of protein in an Amicon Ultra tube (15-mL, 100-kDa MWCO).
19. Centrifuge at 4,000 x g and 4°C for 15 minutes. Refill the tube with more protein eluate, and mix the content by gently inverting the tube 4-5 times.
20. Repeat the centrifugation process to concentrate the eluate down to a final volume of ~2 mL.
21. Transfer the combined Cas9 fractions to an Amicon Ultra tube (15-mL, 100-kDa MWCO).
22. Mix TEV protease to the concentrated eluate to cleave the maltose binding protein fusion and 12x His tag from the recombinant Cas9 protein (1:100 w/w ratio or 10,000 unit (1 mg) TEV protease to 100 mg of target protein measured by the absorbance at 280 nm).
23. Incubate the reaction at 4°C overnight for ~18 hours.
24. Sample 5 μL of the TEV reaction mixture, mix with 40 μL of 1x NuPAGE loading dye in a 1.5-mL microcentrifuge tube, and heat the mixture at 95°C for 5 minutes for SDS-PAGE analysis.

### 4.3. Heparin Ion Exchange Chromatography

1. Inject the protein into the FPLC at 1 mL/min.
2. Combine the peak fractions at ~62 mL retention volume after injection.
3. Transfer the combined Cas9 fractions to a new 50-mL conical tube.
4. Sample 5 μL of the TEV reaction mixture, mix with 40 μL of 1x NuPAGE loading dye in a 1.5-mL microcentrifuge tube, and heat the mixture at 95°C for 5 minutes for SDS-PAGE analysis.

### 4.4. Sterilization, Freezing, and Analysis of Cas9

1. Pre-wet Ultra-free MC centrifugal filters (0.2 μm) with 400 μL of Gel-filtration buffer.
2. Centrifuge at 12,000 x g and 4°C for 1 minute. Discard the flow-through.
3. Transfer Cas9 protein to the centrifugal filters at 400 μL per tube.
4. Centrifuge at 12,000 x g and 4°C for 1 minute. Discard the filter tube.
5. Aliquot Cas9 in 200-μL PCR tubes at 11 μL per tube.
6. Immediately snap-freeze the tube in liquid nitrogen after each pipetting.
7. Store the Cas9 aliquots at -80°C freezer. Avoid repeated freezing and thawing.
8. Dilute 10x Tris-glycine-SDS buffer to 1x in ddH2O.
9. Run a Mini-PROTEAN TGX precast gel (10% PAGE) in Mini-PROTEIN Tetra vertical electrophoresis cell in 1x Tris-glycine-SDS running buffer to analyze the samples collected during the purification.

### 4.5. Enzymatic Synthesis of sgRNA

1. Synthesis of sgRNA transcription template by overlapping PCR.
   - Forward primer: 5'-TAATACGACTCACTATAG-guide sequence-GTTTTAGAGCTATGCTGGAAACAGCATAGCAAGTTAA-3'
   - Reverse primer: 5'-GCACCGACTCGGTGCCACTTTTTCAAGTTGATAACGGACTAGCCTTATTTTAACCTGCTATGCTGTTTCCAGCAT-3'
2. Set up a 30-μL PCR reaction using the KAPA HiFi PCR kit:
   - H2O, molecular biology grade: 18 μL
   - 5x KAPA HiFi buffer: 6 μL
   - 1 mM Top and Bottom premix: 1.5 μL (50 μM each)
   - 10 mM Forward and Reverse premix: 3 μL (1 mM each)
   - KAPA dNTP: 0.9 μL (2 mM)
   - KAPA HiFi polymerase: 0.6 μL (0.6 U)
3. Run PCR in the following thermal cycler setting:
   - 95°C for 3 minutes
   - 95°C for 30 seconds
   - 55°C for 30 seconds
   - 72°C for 1 minute
   - Repeat steps 2-3 for 30 cycles
   - 72°C for 5 minutes
4. Run PCR product on a 2% (w/v) agarose gel in 1x TAE buffer at 100 V.
5. Purify the PCR product using a PCR purification kit.
6. Set up a 150-μL in vitro transcription (IVT) reaction:
   - DEPC-treated H2O: 52 μL
   - 10x IVT buffer: 15 μL
   - 50% PEG 2000: 15 μL
   - 1 M DTT: 3 μL
   - sgRNA template: 30 μL
   - 25 mM NTP mix: 30 μL
   - T7 RNA polymerase: 5 μL
7. Incubate the reaction at 37°C for 2 hours.
8. Add 1.5 μL of RNase-free DNase I and incubate at 37°C for 1 hour to digest the sgRNA DNA template.
9. Mix in 150 μL of 2x Stop solution to terminate the reaction. Store the IVT reaction mixture at -80°C until PAGE purification.
10. Cast a denaturing urea-PAGE gel.
11. Load the IVT reaction mixture onto the gel and run at 200 V for 2 hours.
12. Stain the gel with SYBR Safe according to the manufacturer’s instructions.
13. Excise the sgRNA band and transfer into a 15-mL conical tube.
14. Add 1 mL of 3 M sodium acetate (pH 5) and 7 mL of DEPC-treated water.
15. Add 7 mL of 100% isopropanol and mix well by inverting the tube.
16. Incubate the tube at -20°C overnight to precipitate the sgRNA.
17. Centrifuge at 4,000 x g and 4°C for 15 minutes to pellet the sgRNA. Discard the supernatant.
18. Wash the sgRNA pellet with 1 mL of ice-cold 70% (v/v) ethanol. Centrifuge at 12,000 x g and 4°C for 1 minute. Discard the liquid.
19. Repeat the ethanol wash once.
20. Dry the sgRNA pellet in a SpeedVac vacuum concentrator at room temperature for ~5 minutes.
21. Dissolve the sgRNA pellet in 50 μL of RNA dissolving buffer by incubating at room temperature for 5 minutes.
22. Measure the absorbance of the diluted sgRNA at 260 nm on NanoDrop Lite spectrophotometer.
23. Refold the sgRNA by incubating at 65°C for 5 minutes and cool gradually to room temperature.
24. Snap freeze the sgRNA in 10-μL aliquots in liquid nitrogen and store at -80°C.

### 4.6. Assembly of Cas9 RNP

1. Thaw Cas9 protein (40 μM) and sgRNA (48 μM) to room temperature.
2. Set up 20 μM of Cas9 RNP by mixing equal volumes of Cas9 protein and sgRNA. Note: The molar ratio of Cas9 to sgRNA is 1:1.2. It is crucial to add Cas9 protein to sgRNA, not in reverse order.
3. Swirl the mixture with the pipette tip while slowly releasing Cas9 protein into sgRNA.
4. Pipette mix the solution 4-5 times, and incubate the Cas9 RNP at 37°C for 5 minutes for the formation of ribonucleoprotein complexes.
5. Keep the Cas9 RNP at room temperature and use it within 2 hours.

## 5. Step-by-Step In Situ Hybridization Protocol

# Combined In Situ Hybridization and Immunohistochemistry (ISH/IH) on Free-floating Vibratome Tissue Sections

## 1. Preparation of Mice and Tissue Sections

1. **Obtain Mice**: Acquire mice from appropriate sources in accordance with approved animal regulations.
2. **Euthanize Mouse**: Isolate the whole brain immediately after euthanasia and immerse in freshly prepared paraformaldehyde (PFA) solution. Incubate overnight at 4°C with gentle agitation.
3. **Prepare Vibratome**: Clean the vibratome reservoir and blade holder with RNaseZAP and rinse with RNase-free water. Use a new blade.
4. **Section Brain**: Section the brain in RNase-free PBS buffer at 50 μm thickness using the vibratome.
5. **Transfer Sections**: Transfer vibratome sections to RNase-free Eppendorf tubes filled with PBS.

## 2. Permeabilization and Quenching

1. **Quench Endogenous Peroxidase Activity**: Replace 50% of PBS with hydrogen peroxide in methanol (HPM) and incubate at room temperature for 30 minutes with gentle agitation.
2. **Wash Sections**: Gradually replace HPM with RNase-free PBS in a series of 25%, 50%, and 100% volume exchanges, each for 1-2 minutes.
3. **Block Sections**: Incubate sections in DEPC block at room temperature for 10 minutes, then replace with fresh DEPC block and incubate overnight at 4°C with the primary antibody (e.g., rat anti-CD68 at 1:1,000 dilution).

## 3. In Situ Hybridization (ISH)

1. **Prepare Hybridization Buffer**: Use a buffer containing 50% formamide, 5x SSC, 0.1% Tween-20, 0.5% SDS, 500 μg/ml tRNA, 500 μg/ml salmon sperm DNA, and 50 μg/ml heparin salt.
2. **Denature Probes**: Denature the probes at 100°C for 5 minutes and immediately transfer to ice.
3. **Hybridize Probes**: Incubate hybridized sections overnight at 4°C with anti-Digoxigenin-AP, Fab fragments (1:500 dilution) and anti-rat HRP secondary (1:10,000 dilution).

## 4. Washing and Detection

1. **Wash Sections**: Wash sections 4 times in DEPC block for at least 10 minutes each at room temperature.
2. **Develop NBT/BCIP Reaction**: Follow by developing DAB for colorimetric detection.
3. **Mount Sections**: Mount the sections using aqueous mounting media containing DAPI for nuclear staining.

## 5. Immunohistochemistry (IH)

1. **Primary Antibody Incubation**: Incubate sections in block containing primary antibody (e.g., Calbindin D28K) for 1.5 hours at room temperature.
2. **Secondary Antibody Incubation**: Incubate with secondary antibody conjugated to Alexa Fluor 488 or 594 for 1 hour at 37°C.
3. **Wash Sections**: Rinse with PBS + 0.05% Tween-20 three times for 10 minutes each.

## 6. Imaging

1. **Fluorescent Microscopy**: Visualize Calbindin D28K using fluorescent microscopy.
2. **Overlay Images**: Overlay ISH and IH images using software such as Photoshop or Image J.

## 7. Data Analysis

1. **Image Acquisition**: Use a confocal microscope to acquire images of the stained sections.
2. **Quantitative Analysis**: Analyze the images using software like Fiji or Graphpad Prism to quantify the expression levels and localization of C1q mRNA and protein.

## Notes

- **RNase-Free Environment**: Ensure all steps are performed in an RNase-free environment.
- **Temperature Control**: Maintain appropriate temperatures for hybridization and incubation steps.
- **Antibody Dilutions**: Adjust antibody concentrations based on preliminary experiments for optimal results.
- **Software Use**: Utilize software tools for image overlay and quantitative analysis.

This protocol is adapted from Lopez et al. (2012) and is designed for the detection of complement C1q in neurons and microglia in the mouse brain, combining in situ hybridization for mRNA detection with immunostaining for protein localization.

## 6. Step-by-Step Subcellular Localization Protocol

### Subcellular Localization Using Mouse-Derived Synaptosomes Trypsin Cleavage Assay

#### Materials and Reagents

- Mouse (Mus musculus, C57BL/6J) brain (preferably P60 or above)
- Sucrose buffer
- Trypsin solution (0.1 mg/mL)
- 6x loading sample buffer
- SDS-PAGE gel and electrophoresis equipment
- Western blotting materials (sponge, blotting paper, nitrocellulose membrane, transfer buffer, cool pack)
- Primary antibodies (e.g., GluR1, GluR2, GluN2A, GluN2B, Synaptotagmin1, Synaptophysin1, SNAP-25)
- Blocking solution
- Secondary antibodies
- Ponceau S staining solution
- 1x PBST
- 3% PFA
- PBS
- 2× HBS
- HEK 293T cell growth media
- Lipofectamine 2000 (Thermo Fisher)
- DMEM without phenol red
- Coverslips (15 mm)
- Microscope slides
- Confocal microscope (e.g., Zeiss Axiovert 200 m inverted microscope)
- Image analysis software (e.g., Fiji, GraphPad Prism)

#### Procedure

### 1. Synaptosome Preparation

1. **Dissect Mouse Brain**: Obtain the brain from a mouse (preferably P60 or above) and place it in cold sucrose buffer.
2. **Homogenize Brain Tissue**: Homogenize the brain in cold sucrose buffer using a Dounce homogenizer.
3. **Centrifuge**: Centrifuge the homogenate at 1,000× g for 10 minutes at 4°C to remove nuclei and cell debris.
4. **Collect Supernatant**: Transfer the supernatant to a new tube and centrifuge at 17,000× g for 15 minutes at 4°C.
5. **Resuspend Pellet**: Resuspend the pellet in sucrose buffer and layer on a sucrose gradient.
6. **Ultra-centrifuge**: Ultra-centrifuge at 100,000× g for 1 hour at 4°C to purify synaptosomes.
7. **Collect Synaptosome Fraction**: Collect the synaptosome fraction from the interface and resuspend in sucrose buffer.

### 2. Proteolytic Digestion

1. **Prepare Samples**: For the untreated sample, add 5 mg of crude synaptosomes in a final volume of 10 mL of sucrose buffer without adding trypsin.
2. **Add Trypsin**: To initiate proteolytic digestion, add 500 μL of trypsin solution (0.1 mg/mL) to 5 mg of crude synaptosomes in a final volume of 10 mL of sucrose buffer.
3. **Incubate**: Incubate the untreated sample and the protein–protease mixture for 10 minutes at 30°C in a water bath with occasional gentle inversion.
4. **Centrifuge**: Centrifuge the samples at 8,700× g for 3 minutes at 4°C.
5. **Resuspend Pellets**: Quickly resuspend the pellets in 1 mL of sucrose buffer containing 200 μL of 6x loading sample buffer to stop protease activity.
6. **Boil Samples**: Boil the samples at 95°C for 10 minutes.

### 3. Western Blotting

1. **Prepare Transfer Materials**: Soak sponge, blotting paper, and nitrocellulose membrane in cold 1× transfer buffer.
2. **Assemble Transfer Sandwich**: Assemble the sandwich for the transfer in the following order: sponge—blotting paper—NC membrane—blotting paper—sponge.
3. **Remove Air Bubbles**: Use a roller to gently remove any air bubbles between the layers for even transfer.
4. **Transfer**: Insert the sandwich into the transfer chamber and run the transfer at 110 V for 100 minutes in a cold room or with the chamber on ice.
5. **Stain Membrane**: Stain the membrane with Ponceau S staining solution to visualize protein bands, rinse with water, and take an image.
6. **Block Membrane**: Block the membrane in blocking solution for 30–60 minutes.
7. **Prepare Primary Antibody**: Prepare the primary antibody solution in blocking solution at the required dilution.
8. **Incubate with Primary Antibody**: Incubate the membrane with the primary antibody solution overnight at 4°C.
9. **Wash Membrane**: Wash the membrane three times with 1× PBST.
10. **Incubate with Secondary Antibody**: Incubate with the secondary antibody for 1 hour at room temperature.
11. **Wash Membrane**: Wash the membrane three times with 1× PBST.

### 4. Data Analysis

1. **Image Analysis**: Use Fiji software to analyze the Western blot images.
2. **Quantify Bands**: Measure the intensity of the bands corresponding to the proteins of interest.
3. **Statistical Analysis**: Use GraphPad Prism to perform statistical analysis and generate graphs.

### 5. Confocal Microscopy

1. **Prepare Cells**: Seed HEK 293T cells in a 24-well plate and transfect with plasmids expressing the biosensor and target ligand using Lipofectamine 2000.
2. **Fix Cells**: Replace the media with 3% PFA and fix the cells for 20 minutes at room temperature.
3. **Wash Cells**: Wash the cells three times with PBS.
4. **Stain Cells**: Stain with an antibody recognizing the FP domain of the ATOM biosensor.
5. **Mount Cells**: Mount the coverslips on microscope slides.
6. **Image Cells**: Use a confocal microscope to image the cells, ensuring to use a 12- or 16-bit camera sensor and a 10× objective.
7. **Analyze Images**: Use Fiji software to analyze the images, measuring the fluorescence intensity in specific cellular compartments.

### 6. Validation and Troubleshooting

1. **Positive Control**: Use validated ATOM biosensors and their cognate ligands as a positive control.
2. **Optimize Conditions**: Adjust transfection efficiency and protein expression levels if necessary.
3. **Check Localization**: Verify the subcellular localization of the biosensors by comparing the fluorescence intensity in different compartments.

### 7. Ethical Considerations

- Ensure all animal experiments are reviewed and approved by the ethics committee.
- Follow the committee’s guidelines for animal care and use.

### 8. Pause Points

- Samples can be stored at -20°C for up to three months without degradation.
- Fixed cells can be stored in PBS at 4°C for up to 2 weeks without signal fading.

### 9. Additional Notes

- Ensure all reagents are prepared fresh and stored as per the manufacturer’s instructions.
- Optimize seeding density and DNA:Lipofectamine/cell ratio if using different cell types.

## 7. Step-by-Step Yeast One-Hybrid Assay Protocol

### Yeast One-Hybrid Assay Protocol

#### 1. Constructing and Testing Bait-hSos Fusion Protein

1. Clone DNA encoding the bait protein into the pSos vector using the conventional enzyme-ligation method. Confirm the plasmid construct by DNA sequencing to ensure the correct reading frame between hSos and bait.
2. Test the hSos-bait for auto-activation or interaction with the myristylation signal prior to the CytoTrap two-hybrid screen.
   - Co-transform the pSos bait plasmid with either pMyr or pMyr-Lamin C into yeast host cell cdc25H using the Yeastmaker™ Yeast Transformation System 2.
   - Spread the yeast cells on synthetic media plates without leucine and uracil (SC/-LU) containing glucose and incubate at room temperature for 4-5 days.
   - Pick six independent colonies and transfer to SC/-LU medium plates containing galactose.
   - Incubate the plates at room temperature for 1 hour, then transfer to 36°C for 3 to 6 days.
   - If yeast cells co-transformed with the tested bait plasmid and empty pMyr or pMyr-Lamin C grow at 36°C, the bait plasmid is unsuitable for the CytoTrap two-hybrid system. Only bait plasmids that do not lead to growth at 36°C are suitable for screening interaction partners.

#### 2. Yeast Co-Transformation to Test Bait-Specific Protein-Protein Interaction

1. Transform the purified candidate prey plasmids with either pSos-bait or pSos empty vector into host strain cdc25H.
2. Inoculate the transformants on SC/-LU containing glucose at room temperature for 4 days.
3. Pick three individual colonies and transfer to SC/-LU containing glucose and SC/-LU containing galactose plates.
4. Incubate the plates at 36°C for 3 to 6 days.
5. Only prey plasmids that show specific interaction with pSos-bait, not with pSos empty vector, are kept for sequence analysis.
6. Determine the identity of the cDNA insert by DNA sequencing.

#### 3. Generating a CytoTrap Two-Hybrid cDNA Library

1. Conduct the transformation reactions as suggested by the Yeastmaker™ Yeast Transformation System 2 manual, but perform all yeast culture steps at room temperature to reduce the rate of revert mutations of cdc25H.
2. Spread the transformation reactions on SC/-LU plates (150-mm) containing glucose and incubate at room temperature for 3 to 4 days until colonies grow to 2-3 mm in diameter. Aim for ~3,000 colonies per plate and screen at least 1 million colonies.
3. Replica plate the colonies grown on SC/-LU plates containing glucose to SC/-LU plates containing galactose.
4. Incubate the replica plates at room temperature for 1 hour before moving to 36°C for 3 to 6 days.
5. Pick colonies that grow on SC/-LU plates containing galactose at 36°C when they are 2-3 mm in diameter.
6. Dissolve the picked colonies in 25 μl sterile dH2O and dot 5 μl of the yeast suspension onto four plates: two SC/-LU containing glucose and two SC/-LU containing galactose.
7. Incubate one SC/-LU plate containing glucose and one SC/-LU plate containing galactose at room temperature, and the other two plates at 36°C.
8. Colonies that grow on both galactose and glucose plates at 36°C are temperature revert mutants and should be discarded. Keep colonies that only grow on SC/-LU containing galactose at 36°C as primary positive candidates.

#### 4. DNA Extraction and Purification from Yeast

1. Transfer 1.5 ml of yeast culture to a 1.7-ml centrifuge tube and spin down the cells at 14,000 x g for 1 minute. Remove the supernatant.
2. Add 200 μl of yeast lysis solution to the pellet and resuspend by vortexing.
3. Add 200 μl UltraPure™ phenol:chloroform:isoamyl alcohol (25:24:1, v/v) and 0.3 g of acid-washed glass beads. Vortex vigorously for 2 minutes.
4. Centrifuge at 14,000 x g for 5 minutes at room temperature and transfer the aqueous phase to a new microcentrifuge tube.
5. Add 20 μl of 3 M sodium acetate (pH 5.2) and 500 μl of ice-cold 100% ethanol to the aqueous phase. Incubate at -20°C for 30 minutes.
6. Spin down the pellet at 14,000 x g for 10 minutes at 4°C. Remove the supernatant and wash the pellet with 70% ethanol.
7. Spin down the pellet again at 14,000 x g for 5 minutes at room temperature. Remove the supernatant and dry the pellet at room temperature.
8. Resuspend the pellet in 50 μl sterile ddH2O and use 1-2 μl of yeast plasmid DNA to transform 10-beta Electrocompetent E. coli cells.
9. Spread the transformation reaction on LB/chloramphenicol agar plates and incubate at 37°C overnight.

#### 5. cDNA Library Construction

1. Resuspend the pellet in 8 μl EcoRI adapters and incubate at 4°C for 1 hour to ensure complete dissolution of cDNA.
2. Add 1 μl of 10x T4 ligase buffer and 1 μl of T4 ligase to the tube with cDNA and EcoRI adapters. Incubate the reaction at 16°C overnight.
3. Terminate the reaction by incubating at 70°C for 30 minutes.
4. Cool down the reaction at room temperature for 10 minutes.
5. Add 1 μl of 10x reaction buffer, 8 μl of sterile ddH2O, and 2 μl of T4 polynucleotide kinase. Incubate at 37°C for 1 hour.
6. Terminate the reaction by incubating at 70°C for 30 minutes.
7. Cool the reaction at room temperature for 10 minutes.
8. Add 5 μl of 10x reaction buffer, 22 μl of sterile ddH2O, and 3 μl of Xho I. Incubate at 37°C for 3 hours.
9. Precipitate the cDNA by adding 5 μl of 10x STE buffer and incubate at -20°C overnight.
10. Resuspend the cDNA in 5 μl sterile ddH2O.

#### 6. Ligating cDNA into pMyr Vector and Transforming into E. coli

1. Set up a T4 ligation reaction by adding 2.5 μl of 10x ligase buffer, X μl of resuspended cDNA (1 μg), 5 μl of pMyr vector (precut by EcoRI/XhoI, 500 ng), 2.5 μl of T4 DNA ligase, and ddH2O to a final volume of 25 μl. Incubate overnight at 16°C.
2. Transform all the ligation reaction into XL10-Gold KanR Ultracompetent cells using 2 μl ligation reaction for each transformation according to the manufacturer's instructions.
3. Plate the transformation on 150-mm LB plates containing chloramphenicol (25 μg/ml). Aim for about 50-100 plates in total, with each plate having approximately 30,000 colonies. Incubate at 37°C overnight.

#### 7. Screening and Analysis

1. Pick single colonies and inoculate in LB/chloramphenicol liquid medium at 37°C overnight.
2. Purify the plasmid for further analysis.
3. Perform sequence analysis on the candidate prey plasmids that show specific interaction with pSos-bait.

#### Notes

- Ensure all yeast culture procedures are conducted at room temperature (22-25°C).
- Use the appropriate selective media and incubation conditions to differentiate between true positives and revert mutants.
- Scale up the number of plates if there are more yeast colonies to handle.
- Use the provided recipes for yeast lysis solution, YPDA/YPAD, SC/-LU (glucose and galactose), and other necessary buffers.

1. **Add Stop & Glo® Reagent**: Dispense 50 μL of Stop & Glo® Reagent into the same wells.

1. **Set Up Imaging System**: Use the Stanford Photonics XR/Mega-10 ICCD camera system with Piper software (v. 2.6.17).
2. **Define Regions of Interest**: Ensure each region contains one well of a 96-well plate.
3. **Capture Images**: Capture images and analyze the luminescence intensity.

##### 8. Data Interpretation

1. **Assess Assay Success**: Remove assays that failed to show an increase in F-luc expression with the addition of inducers.
2. **Statistical Analysis**: Perform statistical analysis as needed to interpret the results.

#### Notes

- Ensure all reagents are prepared fresh and stored according to the manufacturer's instructions.

## 9. Step-by-Step RNA-Seq Protocol

# Low-cost and High-throughput RNA-seq Library Preparation for Illumina Sequencing from Plant Tissue

## 1. Preparation of Plant Tissue

### 1.1 Grinding Frozen Plant Tissue

1. Prepare fresh NPB and NPBt buffers as needed (-15 ml NPB and 45 ml NBPt per sample) and cool the buffers on ice.
2. Grind frozen plant tissue (e.g., 50-200 rice root tips) in liquid nitrogen with ceramic mortars and pestles until very fine.
3. Optional: Pour powder into 13 ml Falcon tubes and weigh the sample.
4. Add ~8 ml of ice-cold NPB with a transfer pipette into the mortar and resuspend the powdered tissue by gently grinding it.
5. Filter the resuspension through a 30 μm cell strainer into a 15 ml Falcon tube, and place the Falcon tube on ice while you process the rest of your samples.
6. Centrifuge the filtered nuclei 1,000 x g for 15 min at 4 ℃.

## 2. Isolating Nuclei from Plant Tissue

### 2.1 Washing and Resuspending Nuclei

1. Prepare the streptavidin beads in 1.5 ml tubes (one per sample). Wash 25 μl of Invitrogen M-280 Streptavidin Dynabeads for each sample.
2. Move the Falcon tubes from the centrifuge onto ice.
3. Discard the supernatant.
4. Resuspend the nuclei in 1 ml of cold NPB by pipetting up and down.
5. Set aside 11 μl of the resuspended nuclei for quantification and yield calculations.

### 2.2 Binding Nuclei to Streptavidin Beads

1. Transfer the resuspended nuclei (-1 ml) onto the washed beads (in 1.5 ml tubes).
2. Rotate the tubes on a nutator (~20 rpm) at 4 ℃ for 30 min to bind nuclear envelope biotin onto streptavidin on the beads.
3. Dilute the 1 ml of bead-nuclei mixture with 14 ml NPBt in a 15 ml Falcon tube. Mix gently and place on the nutator (~20 rpm) at 4 ℃ for 30 sec.
4. Place the tube on the magnet at 4 ℃ for 15-20 min.
5. Carefully remove the supernatant and resuspend the beads in 14 ml NPBt.
6. Repeat the wash step twice.
7. Resuspend the beads in 1 ml of NPBt and transfer 1 ml resuspension into a 1.5 ml tube and capture on the magnet.

## 3. RNA Extraction

### 3.1 RNA Isolation

1. Prepare Dynabeads® Oligo(dT)25 by pipetting or gentle vortexing.
2. For each sample, transfer 10 μl well-suspended beads to an RNase-free 1.5-ml microcentrifuge tube and place the tube on a magnet.
3. After 30 s, remove the supernatant.
4. Wash beads with 50-100 μl fresh Lysis/Binding Buffer provided by Dynabeads® Oligo(dT)25 kit.
5. After 30 s, remove the supernatant from the washed Dynabeads.
6. Resuspend the microcentrifuge tube from the magnet in Lysis/Binding Buffer.
7. Incubate at 65℃ in a thermoblock for 5-10 min, with occasional vortexing.

## 4. RNA Cleanup

### 4.1 Using Agencourt RNAClean XP beads

1. Add 1.8 volume (e.g., 77.4 μl for 43 μl of RNA) of RNAClean XP beads to the reaction.
2. Incubate at RT for 10 min.
3. Onto a magnet for 5 min.
4. Remove most of the supernatant (leave 5 μl behind to avoid pulling up beads).
5. Leave tubes on the magnet, add 200 μl 70% ethanol, let stand for 30 sec.
6. Remove all the supernatant.
7. Repeat 70% wash as above once more (two washes total).
8. Remove as much of the EtOH as possible.
9. Air dry beads on the magnet for 10 min (until appear dry).
10. Add 10 μl RNase-free water to the first aliquot dried bead and mix.
11. If you have aliquoted samples, pool back together.
12. Incubate at RT for 5 min.
13. Onto the magnet for 5 min.
14. Recover 10 μl eluate. Store RNA at -80 ℃.

## 6. Library Preparation

### 6.1 Tagmentation Library Preparation

1. Prepare the tagmentation reaction:
   - 10 μl of RNA
   - 2 μl of 10 mM ATP
   - 2 μl of 10× T4 PNK buffer
   - 1 μl of T4 PNK
   - 1 μl of RNase OUT
2. Incubate for 1 h at 37℃ in a thermoblock.
3. Add 159 μl of RNase-free water, 1 μl of GlycoBlue, and 20 μl of 3M NaCl to each sample. Precipitate with 200 μl isopropanol at -20℃ overnight.
4. Briefly vortex and centrifuge at 17,000 × g in a pre-cooled 4℃ tabletop centrifuge for 20 min.
5. Discard supernatant and wash pellet with 800 μl of ice-cold 70% ethanol. Spin again at 17,000 × g and 4℃ for 10 min.
6. Take supernatant off completely and dry pellets at RT for 10–30 min.
7. Resuspend pellet in 12 μl of RNase-free water and dissolve it by incubation at 65℃ in a thermoblock for 5–10 min, with occasional vortexing.

## 9. Notes and Troubleshooting

### 9.1 General Notes

1. Store all reagents at room temperature unless otherwise noted.
2. Perform all steps on ice unless stated otherwise, particularly those affecting mRNA.
3. Ensure all surfaces are clean (e.g., use RNaseZap MRNase Decontamination Solution to clean the bench surface).
4. All tubes and tips that will touch nucleic acids should be nuclease-free grade.

### 9.2 Troubleshooting

1. If RNA yield is low, check the efficiency of the nuclear isolation and appearance of the nuclei under the microscope.
2. Ensure proper mixing and incubation times during the hybridization and DNase I treatment steps.
3. Verify the quality of RNA using a Tape Station D1000 (Agilent Technologies) and ensure RIN values between 7 and 10 for further analysis.

#### Procedure

##### 1. Preparation of Cell Lysates

1. **Cell Culture and Harvesting**
   
   - Grow cells in appropriate culture medium until they reach ~80% confluency.
   - Harvest cells by trypsinization or scraping, then wash with PBS and centrifuge at 1,100 x g for 5 minutes at 4°C.
   - Resuspend the pellet in cold lysis buffer (150 mM NaCl, 1% NP40, 50 mM Tris-HCl pH 7.4) containing protease inhibitors.
   - Incubate on ice for 30 minutes with gentle agitation.
   - Centrifuge at 16,000 x g for 10 minutes at 4°C to remove cell debris. Collect the supernatant as the cell lysate.

2. **Nuclear Extraction (if needed)**
   
   - Resuspend the nuclear pellet in nuclear lysis buffer (20 mM Tris-HCl pH 8.0, 400 mM NaCl, 1 mM EDTA, 10% glycerol, protease inhibitors).
   - Sonicate for 30 seconds with pulses and intervals, then centrifuge at 16,000 x g for 10 minutes at 4°C.
   - Collect the supernatant as the nuclear extract.

##### 2. Immunoprecipitation Setup

1. **Pre-clearing the Lysate**
   
   - Add 50 μl of Protein G agarose beads to the lysate.
   - Incubate for 1 hour at 4°C with gentle rotation.
   - Centrifuge at 10,000 x g for 1 minute and collect the supernatant.

2. **Incubation with Antibody**
   
   - Add the appropriate antibody (e.g., 2-5 μg) to the pre-cleared lysate.
   - Incubate overnight at 4°C with gentle rotation.

3. **Bead Addition**
   
   - Add 50 μl of Protein G agarose beads to the antibody-lysate mixture.
   - Incubate for 2 hours at 4°C with gentle rotation.

##### 3. Washing and Elution

1. **Washing Beads**
   
   - Wash beads 5 times with cold lysis buffer (150 mM NaCl, 50 mM Tris-HCl pH 7.4, 1% NP40) by centrifuging at 10,000 x g for 1 minute at 4°C and removing the supernatant.
   - After the final wash, resuspend beads in 50 μl of 1x Laemmli SDS-PAGE sample buffer.
   - Boil the beads for 5 minutes at 95°C.

2. **Elution**
   
   - Centrifuge at 10,000 x g for 1 minute and collect the supernatant.

##### 4. Sample Analysis

1. **SDS-PAGE and Western Blot**
   - Load the eluted samples onto an SDS-PAGE gel.
   - Transfer proteins to a PVDF membrane.
   - Block the membrane with 5% non-fat milk in TBST (Tris-buffered saline with Tween 20).
   - Incubate with primary antibody specific to the protein of interest.
   - Incubate with HRP-conjugated secondary antibody.
   - Develop using ECL substrate and capture the signal using a chemiluminescent imager.

##### 5. Data Analysis

1. **Western Blot Analysis**
   - Analyze the presence of the protein of interest and its interacting partners.
   - Use appropriate controls, such as a mock IP without antibody, to ensure specificity.

##### Notes

- Ensure all buffers are RNase-free if analyzing RNA-binding proteins.
- Optimize antibody concentration and incubation times based on the specific protein and antibody used.
- Include controls such as a non-specific IgG to assess non-specific binding.
- For high isoelectric point proteins, consider using specialized buffers to maintain protein stability.

This protocol provides a comprehensive guide for performing Co-IP to study protein-protein interactions in cell culture. Adjustments may be necessary based on specific experimental requirements and the nature of the proteins being studied.
